The Problem Nobody Solved

Alcoholics have AA. Drug addicts have NA. Gamblers have GA. Every major addiction in the human catalog has a room you can walk into, a phone number you can call, a sponsor who answers at 2 AM when the bottle or the needle or the casino is whispering your name.

Food addiction has nothing.

No room. No meeting. No chip system. No sponsor. No cultural equivalent with the same weight and seriousness as any other addiction recovery infrastructure. Society does not treat food addiction like a real addiction because society hands you the substance three times a day and calls it normal.

So when the negotiation starts at 9 PM and the refrigerator is humming from ten feet away and every cell in your body is building a case for why this one time is different, who do you call?

Nobody. You sit alone with the voice in your head that sounds exactly like you but is not you. And most of the time, the voice wins. Because the voice never gets tired. The voice never takes a break. The voice has been running this program since you were old enough to reach the refrigerator handle.

That is the gap. That is the infrastructure failure. That is the missing room.

So we built one.

Why AI for Addiction Recovery
